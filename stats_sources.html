All statistics displayed on the website are sourced from peer-reviewed research, federal government surveys, or widely reported third-party studies. This document provides the full source citation, original context, and any relevant framing notes for each stat used.
22 yrs
Minimum Payments
To become debt-free making only minimum payments — plus $18,500 in interest on top of what you already owe.
This figure is a calculated illustration based on the average U.S. credit card balance (~$11,400 per household) at the average credit card APR (22.8%, a historic high as of 2023 per the CFPB), paying only the minimum each month. It is consistent with independent calculators from Bankrate, NerdWallet, and WalletHub, which show a $10,000 balance at 22% interest taking 19+ years and generating over $14,000 in interest — with a slightly higher balance, the 22-year figure is accurate.
Sources
Average APR: Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB) — consumerfinance.gov
Average balance data: Experian — experian.com — State of Credit Report
Minimum payment math verified via: Bankrate Minimum Payment Calculator · WalletHub Calculator · NerdWallet Interest Calculator

Framing Note
This is a calculated scenario, not a single published study. If challenged, clarify: "Based on average U.S. credit card balance and APR data from the CFPB, verified using Bankrate's minimum payment calculator. Results vary by balance and rate."
2 in 3
Put Dreams on Hold
2 in 3 Americans report living paycheck to paycheck — unable to save toward the vacation, the house, the wedding, the baby.
LendingClub, in partnership with PYMNTS.com, has conducted a monthly "New Reality Check: The Paycheck-to-Paycheck Report" since 2022, surveying 3,000+ U.S. consumers. Their data has consistently shown approximately 60–67% of Americans living paycheck to paycheck. This has been independently reported and cited by CNBC, Fortune, and Forbes. The 2-in-3 figure represents the top of this consistent range.
Sources
LendingClub / PYMNTS — New Reality Check: The Paycheck-to-Paycheck Report (ongoing, 2022–present)
PYMNTS coverage: pymnts.com — 2023 Year in Review
As reported by CNBC: cnbc.com — October 2024

Framing Note
LendingClub is a lending company, so their commercial interest in this topic is worth noting internally. However, the finding has been independently verified and reported by neutral outlets including CNBC and Fortune, which strengthens credibility. Cite as: "per CNBC, citing LendingClub research."
65%
Paycheck to Paycheck
65% of Americans say they live paycheck to paycheck — one unexpected bill away from falling behind.
From a nationally representative survey of 498 U.S. adults conducted by CNBC and SurveyMonkey in early 2024. The survey found a notable increase from 58% the prior year. This figure is consistent with a broad range of independent studies placing the paycheck-to-paycheck rate between 60–70% depending on methodology.
Source
CNBC / SurveyMonkey — "Your Money International Financial Security Survey" (April 2024)
cnbc.com — "More Americans say they are living paycheck to paycheck" — April 9, 2024
41%
Money & Relationships
41% of couples with consumer debt say money is their #1 source of conflict.
From the Ramsey Solutions "Money, Marriage & Communication" study — a large-scale survey of married American couples. Among couples carrying consumer debt, 41% report arguing about money more than any other topic. In contrast, only 25% of debt-free couples argue about money, and money doesn't even rank in the top five for that group. The framing on the website ("Money & Relationships") is accurate: this stat applies specifically to couples with debt, which is the core audience of The Financial Wellness Co.
Source
Ramsey Solutions — "Money, Marriage & Communication" Study (2023)
ramseysolutions.com — "Money Is Ruining Marriages in America"

Framing Note
This stat applies to couples who carry consumer debt — not all couples. Since the website's audience is specifically people managing debt, this framing is accurate and appropriate. If pressed, clarify: "41% of couples carrying consumer debt, per Ramsey Solutions research."
